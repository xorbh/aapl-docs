## Purpose

This guide helps you select the correct material for manufacturing requirements. Use the decision matrix to identify the best material based on application requirements.

---

## Material Selection Decision Matrix

**How to use**: Find your primary requirement, read across to get recommended materials in priority order.

### By Application Type

| Application | 1st Choice | 2nd Choice | 3rd Choice | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **General mechanical parts** | AISI 1018 Steel | 6061-T6 Aluminum | Brass | High carbon steel |
| **Structural strength** | AISI 4140 Steel | 7075-T73 Aluminum | Alloy steel | Pure aluminum |
| **Corrosion resistance** | 303 Stainless | 316 Stainless | Brass | Mild steel (uncoated) |
| **Wear resistance** | Tool steel (A2) | 4140 Steel | Hardened 1045 | Low carbon steel |
| **High temperature** | Stainless 321 | Stainless 347 | Inconel (if budget) | Aluminum |
| **Non-magnetic** | 300 Series Stainless | Aluminum | Brass | Carbon steel |
| **Electrical conduction** | Copper | Brass | Aluminum | Stainless steel |
| **Cost-sensitive** | Mild Steel 1018 | 6061 Aluminum | Brass | Tool steel |
| **Easy to machine** | Brass | 6061 Aluminum | 12L14 Steel | Tool steel |
| **High precision required** | Tool Steel (A2) | 4140 Steel | Stainless | Cast iron |

## Common Materials Specifications

### Steel Grades

#### Mild Steel (AISI 1018)
- **Carbon content**: 0.15-0.20%
- **Hardness**: Low (Brinell 126)
- **Tensile strength**: 63,800 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 53,700 PSI
- **Machinability**: Excellent
- **Welding**: Excellent
- **Cost**: $0.50-0.75/lb
- **Applications**: General parts, fasteners, simple structures
- **Availability**: Excellent (stock everywhere)
- **Notes**: Easy to machine and weld, not for high-load applications

#### Medium Carbon Steel (AISI 1045)
- **Carbon content**: 0.42-0.50%
- **Hardness**: Medium (Brinell 180)
- **Tensile strength**: 96,000 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 60,000 PSI
- **Machinability**: Good
- **Welding**: Fair (preheating required)
- **Cost**: $0.60-0.85/lb
- **Applications**: Shafts, gears, mechanical parts
- **Notes**: Higher strength than 1018, must be hardened for wear

#### High Carbon Steel (AISI 1095)
- **Carbon content**: 0.90-1.03%
- **Hardness**: High (Brinell 250)
- **Tensile strength**: 131,000 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 75,000 PSI
- **Machinability**: Fair (difficult)
- **Welding**: Poor
- **Cost**: $0.70-1.00/lb
- **Applications**: Cutting tools, springs, wear surfaces
- **Notes**: Very hard, brittle, difficult to machine

#### Alloy Steel (AISI 4140)
- **Carbon content**: 0.38-0.43%
- **Alloy content**: Chromium, molybdenum
- **Hardness**: High (Brinell 262)
- **Tensile strength**: 125,000+ PSI
- **Yield strength**: 85,000+ PSI
- **Machinability**: Fair
- **Welding**: Fair
- **Cost**: $1.00-1.50/lb
- **Applications**: High-stress parts, axles, shafts, tooling
- **Notes**: Excellent strength/weight, responds well to heat treatment

#### Stainless Steel 303
- **Type**: Austenitic
- **Corrosion resistance**: Excellent
- **Tensile strength**: 95,000 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 40,000 PSI
- **Machinability**: Excellent (easy to machine)
- **Welding**: Fair
- **Cost**: $2.00-3.00/lb
- **Applications**: Food equipment, sanitary parts, corrosion-resistant parts
- **Notes**: Magnetic properties similar to steel

#### Stainless Steel 304
- **Type**: Austenitic
- **Corrosion resistance**: Excellent (general purpose)
- **Tensile strength**: 85,000 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 30,000 PSI
- **Machinability**: Fair (work hardens)
- **Welding**: Excellent
- **Cost**: $1.50-2.50/lb
- **Applications**: Sinks, decorative, general corrosion resistance
- **Notes**: Most common stainless, tough to machine

#### Stainless Steel 316
- **Type**: Austenitic
- **Corrosion resistance**: Excellent (marine/chemical)
- **Tensile strength**: 84,000 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 30,000 PSI
- **Machinability**: Fair
- **Welding**: Excellent
- **Cost**: $2.50-3.50/lb
- **Applications**: Marine equipment, chemical handling, high corrosion
- **Notes**: Better than 304 for harsh environments

#### Tool Steel (AISI A2)
- **Hardness**: Very high (Brinell 293)
- **Tensile strength**: 100,000+ PSI (annealed)
- **Machinability**: Poor (when hardened)
- **Wear resistance**: Excellent
- **Cost**: $3.00-5.00/lb
- **Applications**: Dies, molds, punches, hard wear surfaces
- **Notes**: Must be hardened, difficult to machine

### Aluminum Alloys

#### 6061-T6 (Most Common)
- **Tensile strength**: 45,000 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 40,000 PSI
- **Hardness**: Medium (Brinell 95)
- **Machinability**: Excellent
- **Welding**: Good
- **Corrosion resistance**: Good
- **Cost**: $0.80-1.20/lb
- **Applications**: General purpose, structural, extrusions
- **Notes**: Most common aluminum, good balance of properties

#### 7075-T73 (High Strength)
- **Tensile strength**: 73,000 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 63,000 PSI
- **Hardness**: High (Brinell 150)
- **Machinability**: Fair (slightly difficult)
- **Welding**: Fair (not recommended)
- **Corrosion resistance**: Fair
- **Cost**: $1.50-2.50/lb
- **Applications**: Aerospace, high-stress parts, structural
- **Notes**: Very strong for weight, expensive

#### 2024-T4 (Aircraft Quality)
- **Tensile strength**: 70,000 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 50,000 PSI
- **Hardness**: High (Brinell 120)
- **Machinability**: Fair
- **Welding**: Not recommended
- **Corrosion resistance**: Fair (needs coating)
- **Cost**: $2.00-3.00/lb
- **Applications**: Aircraft, high-stress aerospace
- **Notes**: Expensive, requires special handling

#### 1100 (Pure Aluminum)
- **Tensile strength**: 13,000 PSI
- **Yield strength**: 5,000 PSI
- **Hardness**: Very low (Brinell 23)
- **Machinability**: Excellent
- **Welding**: Excellent
- **Corrosion resistance**: Excellent
- **Cost**: $0.60-1.00/lb
- **Applications**: Decorative, chemical containers, soft parts
- **Notes**: Very soft, low strength, excellent for non-load bearing

---

### Other Common Materials

#### Brass (Cu-Zn)
- **Tensile strength**: 50,000-60,000 PSI
- **Machinability**: Excellent (best)
- **Corrosion resistance**: Excellent
- **Welding**: Fair (brazing preferred)
- **Cost**: $1.50-2.50/lb
- **Magnetic**: Non-magnetic
- **Applications**: Decorative, low-friction, gears
- **Notes**: Chips easily (no coolant often needed)

#### Copper
- **Tensile strength**: 32,000 PSI
- **Machinability**: Fair (tough, sticky)
- **Electrical conduction**: Excellent
- **Corrosion resistance**: Good
- **Cost**: $3.00-4.00/lb
- **Applications**: Electrical, thermal, decorative
- **Notes**: Expensive, sticky chips, excellent conductor

#### Cast Iron
- **Tensile strength**: 20,000-60,000 PSI
- **Hardness**: Medium (Brinell 140-200)
- **Machinability**: Fair (abrasive, brittle)
- **Welding**: Poor
- **Corrosion resistance**: Fair
- **Cost**: $0.40-0.80/lb
- **Applications**: Engine blocks, frames, heavy structures
- **Notes**: Brittle, inexpensive, heavy

## Heat Treatment Impact

### Before vs. After Heat Treatment

| Process | Material | Before | After | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hardening** | 1045 Steel | 96,000 PSI | 140,000+ PSI | Becomes brittle |
| **Tempering** | Hardened steel | Very brittle | Tough + hard | Reduces hardness |
| **Annealing** | 1095 Steel | Hard, brittle | Soft, ductile | Allows machining |
| **Stress Relief** | Any steel | High residual stress | Relieved | Prevents distortion |
| **Aging** | 7075 Aluminum | Soft | Strong (73,000 PSI) | T73 means aged |

## Quick Selection Matrix

**Answer these questions:**

1. **Strength required?**
   - Low → 6061 Aluminum or Brass
   - Medium → Mild Steel or 304 Stainless
   - High → 4140 Steel or 7075 Aluminum

2. **Must resist corrosion?**
   - Yes → Stainless 304/316 or Aluminum
   - No → Steel or Brass

3. **Must be easy to machine?**
   - Yes → Brass or 6061 Aluminum
   - No → Stainless or 4140 Steel

4. **Budget constraint?**
   - Lowest cost → Mild Steel
   - Moderate → 6061 Aluminum
   - Flexible → Choose by performance

5. **Must be non-magnetic?**
   - Yes → 300-series Stainless or Aluminum
   - No → Steel options
