# WI-002: Material Receiving & Inspection

**Document ID**: WI-002
**Effective Date**: 2024-11-26
**Last Revised**: 2024-11-26
**Author**: Quality & Procurement
**Approval**: Quality Manager

---

## Purpose & Scope

This work instruction establishes procedures for receiving, inspecting, and storing raw materials and components. All material received must be verified against purchase orders and quality specifications before acceptance into inventory.

**Material Types Covered**:
- Raw material (steel, aluminum, brass, etc.)
- Semi-finished stock
- Purchased components and assemblies
- Consumables (coolant, belts, etc.)
- Tooling and fixtures

**Personnel**: Receiving clerk, Quality Inspector, Warehouse Staff

---

## Before You Start

### Required Training & Certification
- [ ] Completed Material Handling Training
- [ ] Familiar with Inventory Management System
- [ ] Completed Quality Inspection Training
- [ ] Knows SDS (Safety Data Sheet) locations

### Required Materials & Tools
- Purchase Order (PO) printed or accessible
- Material Inspection Checklist
- Precision measurement tools (calipers, straightedge)
- Inspection samples/gages if required
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for hazardous materials
- Material labels and traceability information
- Photography equipment (for damage documentation)

## Step 1: Receiving Documentation Review (5 minutes)

### Check Delivery Receipt Against PO

1. Locate original purchase order for delivery
2. Compare delivery documentation to PO:
   - [ ] Supplier name matches PO
   - [ ] PO number on delivery receipt
   - [ ] Delivery address correct
   - [ ] Date received recorded
3. Check delivery receipt for signatures:
   - Driver signature present
   - Receiving clerk initials present
   - Time of delivery recorded
4. Verify materials listed on receipt

### Verify Order Quantities

1. Count packaging units (boxes, rolls, pallets)
2. Compare unit count to PO ordered quantity
3. Check weight if listed on PO:
   - Scale material if large quantities
   - Record weight on receiving form
4. Document any quantity discrepancies immediately
5. Take photograph if quantity mismatch

---

## Step 2: Packaging Integrity Inspection (10 minutes)

### External Packaging Assessment

1. Inspect all packaging for visible damage:
   - [ ] Boxes crushed, torn, or water damaged
   - [ ] Crates broken or missing boards
   - [ ] Pallet cracked or broken
   - [ ] Strapping loose or broken
   - [ ] Seals broken or tampered with
2. Check for signs of environmental exposure:
   - [ ] Water stains or moisture
   - [ ] Rust or corrosion on packaging
   - [ ] Freeze damage (ice crystals)
   - [ ] Extreme temperature damage
3. Verify product labels are readable:
   - [ ] Part numbers visible
   - [ ] Manufacturer name clear
   - [ ] Expiration date visible (if applicable)
   - [ ] Lot/batch number visible
4. Document condition with notation and photo if damaged

### Material Condition Assessment

1. Open packaging carefully
2. Visually inspect exposed material:
   - [ ] No visible cracks or breaks
   - [ ] No corrosion or discoloration
   - [ ] No contamination or debris
   - [ ] Surface quality acceptable
3. Check for signs of improper storage:
   - [ ] Moisture intrusion
   - [ ] Temperature damage
   - [ ] Mechanical damage in transit
4. Note any concerns for quality inspector

---

## Step 3: Traceability Information Collection (5 minutes)

### Required Traceability Data

Collect and document the following information:

1. **Supplier Information**:
   - Supplier name and address
   - Supplier contact person
   - Delivery date and time
   - Invoice number (if applicable)

2. **Material Identification**:
   - Part number (manufacturer and our part number)
   - Material type or grade
   - Quantity received
   - Unit of measure (pieces, pounds, meters, etc.)

3. **Lot/Batch Traceability**:
   - Lot number or batch number
   - Manufacturing date (if available)
   - Expiration date (if applicable)
   - Mill certificate or CoC number

4. **Shipping Documentation**:
   - Bill of lading number
   - Packing slip number
   - Any carrier information

5. **Certifications**:
   - Certificate of Conformance (CoC) present
   - Material certification (mill cert, test reports)
   - Special certifications (AS9100, etc.)

---

## Step 4: Quality Sample Inspection (15-30 minutes)

### Sampling Plan

**For New Suppliers**:
- Inspect 100% of first delivery from new supplier
- Reduce to 10% sample after 5 successful deliveries

**For Established Suppliers**:
- Inspect minimum 10% of each delivery (minimum 5 pieces)
- Increase to 100% if defects found

**For Critical Materials** (structural, safety-critical):
- Inspect 100% of all deliveries
- Additional testing may be required

### Visual Sample Inspection

1. Select sample items randomly from delivery
2. Visually inspect each sample:
   - [ ] No cracks, chips, or breaks
   - [ ] No corrosion, rust, or discoloration
   - [ ] Surface finish acceptable
   - [ ] No dimensional anomalies visible
   - [ ] No contamination
3. Document visual findings on form
4. If visual inspection fails, reject delivery

### Dimensional Sample Inspection

**For Metal Stock**:
1. Use calipers to verify stock diameter/dimensions
2. Measure minimum 3 pieces from different areas of delivery
3. Compare to specification:
   - [ ] Dimensions within tolerance
   - [ ] Surface finish acceptable
   - [ ] No deformation or bending
4. Record measurements on inspection form

**For Fasteners & Bolts**:
1. Verify head markings match specification
2. Check threads with thread gage if available
3. Verify length with calipers
4. Check for plating or coating:
   - [ ] Coating uniform and complete
   - [ ] No bare spots or damage
5. Record findings on form

**For Components & Assemblies**:
1. Verify all components present
2. Check assembly alignment
3. Verify fit and movement if applicable
4. Check for damage or wear
5. Verify markings and serial numbers if required

### Hardness/Property Verification (if required)

1. Check if material certification requires hardness testing
2. For critical materials, perform hardness test:
   - Use calibrated hardness tester
   - Test minimum 2 samples
   - Compare to specification
3. Document test results and pass/fail status
4. If hardness out of range, reject material

### Documentation Review

1. Verify Certificate of Conformance (CoC) present:
   - [ ] CoC signed by supplier
   - [ ] Test data included
   - [ ] Material properties listed
   - [ ] Lot number matches delivery
2. Review any test reports or certifications:
   - [ ] Signature and date present
   - [ ] Test data acceptable
   - [ ] Within specification limits
3. Attach all certifications to receiving packet

## Step 5: Acceptance/Rejection Decision (5 minutes)

### Acceptance Criteria

Material is **ACCEPTED** if:
- ✓ Quantity matches purchase order
- ✓ Visual inspection passes
- ✓ Dimensional inspection passes
- ✓ All certifications present and valid
- ✓ Packaging integrity acceptable
- ✓ Traceability information complete

### Conditional Acceptance Criteria

Material may be **CONDITIONALLY ACCEPTED** if:
- Minor packaging damage with no material damage
- Minor dimensional deviation within manufacturing tolerance
- Certification slightly delayed but supplier provides verbal confirmation

**Note**: Conditionally accepted material must be clearly marked and segregated. Quality Manager must review before use.

### Rejection Criteria

Material is **REJECTED** if:
- Quantity does not match PO
- Visual defects present (cracks, corrosion, contamination)
- Dimensional inspection fails
- Hardness/property verification fails
- Certifications missing or invalid
- Packaging damage extends to material
- Material past expiration date

## Step 6: Material Acceptance & Storage (10-20 minutes)

### For Accepted Material

1. **Apply Acceptance Label**:
   - Create "ACCEPTED" label with date
   - Include receiving date
   - Include lot number
   - Include storage location

2. **Generate Inventory Records**:
   - Enter material into inventory system
   - Record lot number in system
   - Record quantity received
   - Record unit of measure
   - Note supplier and invoice number

3. **Prepare for Storage**:
   - Clean material surface if needed (dry cloth only)
   - Do not apply solvents or aggressive cleaners
   - Keep in original packaging if protective
   - Remove from packaging if damaged (seal in new packaging)

4. **Transport to Storage**:
   - Use appropriate equipment (forklift, pallet jack)
   - Do not drop or slide material
   - Secure load to prevent shifting
   - Transport on designated material path (avoid production floor)

### For Conditionally Accepted Material

1. **Apply "CONDITIONAL ACCEPTANCE" Label**:
   - Clearly mark as conditional
   - Note reason for conditional status
   - Include required approval signature
   - Flag for Quality Manager review

2. **Segregate in Storage**:
   - Store separately from accepted material
   - Place in quarantine area if available
   - Mark with yellow "CAUTION" tape
   - Do not release to production without approval

---

## Step 7: Rejected Material Handling (10 minutes)

### For Rejected Material

1. **Apply Rejection Label**:
   - Mark as "REJECTED - DO NOT USE"
   - Document reason for rejection
   - Include date of rejection
   - Include inspector name/signature

2. **Segregate Material**:
   - Move to quarantine/rejected area
   - Place away from production materials
   - Secure with "DO NOT USE" sign or tape

3. **Document Rejection**:
   - Complete Material Non-Conformance Report (NCR)
   - Include photographs if applicable
   - Document measurement results
   - Note inspection findings
   - Include corrective action needed

4. **Notify Supplier**:
   - Send rejection notification to supplier
   - Include NCR number and findings
   - Request replacement shipment
   - Request credit memo if applicable
   - Communicate expected resolution timeline

5. **Arrange Return**:
   - Prepare material for return shipment
   - Repackage if necessary
   - Coordinate with supplier on pickup
   - Document return authorization number
   - Update inventory system to remove rejected material

---

## Step 8: Material Storage & Organization (Ongoing)

### Storage Location Assignment

1. Assign storage location based on material type:
   - **Steel Stock**: Bin location S-001 to S-050
   - **Aluminum Stock**: Bin location A-001 to A-025
   - **Fasteners/Hardware**: Bin location F-001 to F-030
   - **Specialty Materials**: Bin location SP-001 to SP-010

2. Organize by:
   - Material type (keep similar materials together)
   - Lot number (keep same lot together)
   - Size/dimension (organize largest to smallest for accessibility)

3. Update location in inventory system

### Storage Conditions

**Temperature & Humidity**:
- Maintain 65-75°F ambient temperature
- Maintain 40-50% relative humidity
- Prevent exposure to direct sunlight
- Avoid condensation on material

**Organization**:
- Store material on clean, dry pallets
- Use racking system for vertical storage
- Keep aisles clear for access
- Do not store heavy materials on top of delicate items

**Safety**:
- Keep hazardous materials in locked area
- Secure tall stacks to prevent tipping
- Keep floor area dry to prevent slipping
- Remove excess packaging/waste

## Step 9: Receiving Documentation Completion (5 minutes)

### Final Documentation

1. Complete Receiving Form:
   - [ ] PO number
   - [ ] Supplier name
   - [ ] Delivery date
   - [ ] Quantity received
   - [ ] Material condition assessment
   - [ ] Inspection results (pass/fail)
   - [ ] Acceptance/rejection status
   - [ ] Storage location assigned
   - [ ] Inspector signature and date
   - [ ] Receiving clerk signature and date

2. File Documentation:
   - Original receiving form filed by PO number
   - Attach CoC/certifications to file
   - Attach inspection notes/photos
   - Attach NCR if rejected

3. Update System:
   - Verify inventory system updated
   - Confirm lot number recorded
   - Confirm storage location set
   - Verify material status is "Available" or "Conditional"

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: Delivery Doesn't Match PO
- [ ] Check for split deliveries (partial shipment)
- [ ] Verify PO number on documentation
- [ ] Check supplier invoice for clarification
- [ ] Contact supplier immediately
- [ ] Document discrepancy and notify procurement

### Problem: Certifications Missing
- [ ] Request CoC from supplier immediately by phone/email
- [ ] Conditionally accept if supplier confirms documents in transit
- [ ] Do not release material until certifications received
- [ ] If not received in 5 business days, reject and return material

### Problem: Material Visually Damaged
- [ ] Take photographs of damage
- [ ] Do not remove material from packaging if possible
- [ ] Reject material immediately
- [ ] Prepare for return to supplier
- [ ] Notify procurement of issue

### Problem: Packaging Water Damaged
- [ ] Open packaging carefully
- [ ] Inspect material inside thoroughly
- [ ] Dry material if wet (compressed air or cloths)
- [ ] Allow to air dry if moisture present
- [ ] Conditional acceptance if material undamaged
- [ ] Document condition with photos

## Safety Reminders

- **Lift properly**: Bend knees, keep back straight when lifting
- **Use equipment**: Use forklifts and pallet jacks for heavy materials
- **Protect eyes**: Wear safety glasses when inspecting material
- **Know limits**: Ask for help if load exceeds 50 pounds
- **Watch for hazards**: Keep floor clear of slipping hazards
- **Report unsafe material**: Notify supervisor of hazardous material concerns
