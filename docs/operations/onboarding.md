# Employee Onboarding

## Overview

Our comprehensive onboarding program ensures new employees understand company culture, expectations, processes, and their specific role within the organization. This document outlines the onboarding timeline and required training for all new manufacturing employees.

## Day 1: Welcome & Orientation

### Morning - HR Department

**Duration**: 2 hours

**Topics Covered**:
- Welcome to company (CEO/management greeting video)
- Company history, values, and mission
- Organizational structure
- Employee handbook review
- Benefits overview (health insurance, 401k, etc.)
- Payroll and timekeeping systems
- Building access procedures (badges, parking)

**Documents to Complete**:
- Tax withholding forms (W-4)
- Direct deposit authorization
- Insurance beneficiary designations
- Confidentiality agreement
- Acknowledgment of handbook receipt

### Afternoon - Department/Manager

**Duration**: 2 hours

**Activities**:
- Meet department manager and team members
- Facility tour (production floor, restrooms, emergency exits)
- Safety briefing overview
- Review job description and expectations
- Meet assigned mentor/buddy
- Review first week schedule

### End of Day

- [ ] Issued employee ID badge
- [ ] Provided employee handbook (printed)
- [ ] Scheduled orientation sessions
- [ ] Confirmed start time for Day 2

## Day 2: Department Onboarding

### Morning - Department Specific

**Duration**: 2-3 hours

**Topics**:
- Department organizational structure
- Key personnel and their roles
- Department processes and workflows
- Equipment overview (location and basic purpose)
- Work area tour and workspace assignment
- Communication methods and preferences
- Break room and facility amenities

### Afternoon - Role Specific Training

**Duration**: 2-3 hours

**Topics**:
- Job responsibilities and expectations
- Performance metrics and evaluation criteria
- Reporting structure and chain of command
- Key projects and priorities
- Required documentation and sign-offs
- Questions and clarification

### Mentor/Buddy Assignment

- Assign experienced employee as mentor
- Schedule 30-minute introduction
- Provide mentor with onboarding checklist
- Explain support role and expectations

## Days 3-5: Mandatory Safety Training

### Day 3: Safety Fundamentals (4 hours)

**Topics**:
- OSHA regulations overview
- Workplace hazards in manufacturing
- Hazard identification and reporting
- Personal protective equipment (PPE) requirements
- Emergency procedures and evacuation routes
- Incident reporting procedures
- Company safety policies

**Materials**:
- Safety handbook
- Facility hazard map
- Emergency contact list
- OSHA poster review

**Assessment**:
- Written safety quiz
- Acknowledgment of safety training

### Day 4: Equipment Safety (4 hours)

**Topics**:
- Equipment safety features and guards
- Proper lifting and ergonomics
- Machine lockout/tagout (LOTO) overview
- Tool safety and usage
- Confined space awareness
- Hot work procedures (if applicable)
- Chemical safety basics

**Hands-on**:
- Equipment inspection walk-through
- Guard inspection and testing
- PPE fitting (safety shoes, glasses, etc.)

### Day 5: Procedures & Emergency Response (3 hours)

**Topics**:
- Fire safety and extinguisher use (PASS method)
- Emergency response procedures
- Medical emergency protocols
- Chemical spill response
- First aid station locations
- Incident investigation procedures
- Reporting systems and hotlines

**Drills**:
- Participate in facility evacuation drill
- Review emergency assembly area
- Meet first aid responders

## Weeks 2-3: Systems & Procedures Training

### Production Management System (Day 6-7)

**Topics**:
- System login and navigation
- Work order tracking and status
- Time tracking and labor entry
- Data entry requirements and accuracy
- Report generation and access
- System troubleshooting

**Hands-on**:
- Practice with test account
- Submit sample work order
- Generate standard reports

### Quality Standards (Day 8-9)

**Topics**:
- Inspection procedures and requirements
- Defect classification (critical, major, minor)
- Documentation and record keeping
- Quality tools and measurement
- Non-conformance reporting
- First article inspection overview

**Training**:
- Walk through inspection station
- Practice with sample parts
- Review past inspection records

### Inventory & Material Handling (Day 10)

**Topics**:
- Inventory management system
- Material requisition procedures
- FIFO (First-In, First-Out) rotation
- Storage location assignments
- Hazardous material handling (if applicable)
- Cycle counting procedures

**Activities**:
- Tour warehouse/storage areas
- Practice material requisition
- Observe cycle count

### Equipment Maintenance Basics (Day 11)

**Topics**:
- Daily equipment checks
- Lubrication requirements
- Filter and fluid changes overview
- Cleaning and maintenance
- Maintenance request procedures
- When to stop and report equipment issues

### Safety Protocols Deep Dive (Day 12)

**Topics**:
- LOTO detailed procedures
- Energy isolation methods
- Tag-out procedures and authorization
- Confined space entry requirements
- Hot work permits and procedures
- Respirator fit-testing and usage

**Hands-on**:
- Practice LOTO steps on demonstration equipment
- Respirator fit-testing (if required)

## Week 4: Practical Hands-On Training

### Days 15-17: Equipment Shadowing

- Shadow experienced operator for 3 days
- Observe machine setup and operation
- Learn equipment-specific procedures
- Practice under direct supervision
- Ask questions and clarify procedures

### Days 18-19: First Production Run

- Operate equipment with supervisor present
- Perform setup procedures independently
- Execute production run with monitoring
- Document production data correctly
- Handle quality checkpoints
- Complete end-of-shift procedures

### Day 20: Competency Assessment

**Written Exam** (1 hour):
- Safety procedures (pass score: 90%)
- Equipment operation (pass score: 85%)
- Quality standards (pass score: 85%)
- Company procedures (pass score: 80%)

**Practical Assessment**:
- Equipment setup demonstration
- Production cycle execution
- Quality inspection accuracy
- Safety procedures demonstration

**Sign-Off**:
- Supervisor certification of competency
- Safety sign-off
- Manager approval

## Ongoing Onboarding (Weeks 5+)

### 30-Day Check-In (Week 6)

- Manager review of progress
- Address any concerns or challenges
- Adjust training if needed
- Informal feedback discussion
- Confirm understanding of role

### 60-Day Review (Week 8)

- Formal performance discussion
- Review key metrics (safety, quality, attendance)
- Discuss strengths and areas for improvement
- Update training plan if needed
- Career development conversation

### 90-Day Evaluation (Week 13)

- Formal performance evaluation
- Assessment against job description
- Review safety record
- Evaluate quality performance
- Determine employment status confirmation
- Discuss future opportunities

## Mentor/Buddy Program

### Mentor Responsibilities

- Provide guidance on daily tasks
- Answer questions and clarify procedures
- Model proper safety procedures
- Provide constructive feedback
- Report on new hire progress
- Be available for 4+ weeks of onboarding

### Check-in Schedule

- **Daily (Week 1)**: Beginning and end of day
- **Twice Weekly (Week 2-3)**: Morning and mid-day
- **Weekly (Week 4+)**: At least once per week
- **Ad-hoc**: As needed for specific questions

### Mentor Evaluation

New hire receives feedback about mentor quality:
- Does mentor explain procedures clearly?
- Is mentor patient and supportive?
- Does mentor answer questions adequately?
- Would you recommend this mentor to others?

## Success Metrics

New hire is considered successfully onboarded when:

- **Safety**: Zero safety incidents during first 90 days
- **Quality**: Defect rate within acceptable limits
- **Production**: Meets productivity targets for role
- **Attendance**: 100% attendance after first week (excluding approved absences)
- **Knowledge**: Passes all required training assessments
- **Teamwork**: Demonstrates cooperation and communication
- **Compliance**: All documentation complete and signed

## FAQ

**Q: What if an employee doesn't pass the competency assessment?**
A: Employee receives additional training on failed areas and retakes assessment. Multiple failures may result in role reassignment or termination.

**Q: Can onboarding timeline be accelerated?**
A: Safety training must be completed before independent operation. Other training may be compressed for experienced hires, subject to manager approval.

**Q: What happens after 90 days?**
A: Successful employees transition to regular probationary period (if applicable) with standard management. Continued development tracked in annual reviews.

**Q: How is onboarding tracked?**
A: Onboarding checklist maintained by HR and manager. Progress reviews at 30/60/90 days documented in personnel file.
